How do I get started?

Print out your own template here

People in your district are successful 67% of the time that they appeal their ticket! By starting the appeal process you increase your chances from 0%.

What you should have with you:

  • Ticket number
  • Pictures of signs, documents, drivers license
  • Description of surrounding, street names, and time

Keep in mind:

  • You have 7 days to contest a ticket. You can submit evidence or request an in-person hearing online at City of Chicago, or by mail to:
  • City of Chicago
  • Department of Finance
  • P.O. Box 88292
  • Chicago, IL 60680-1292
  • If no action is taken in 7 days, a notice will be sent that lets you know you have 21 days to contest the ticket in-person. After 21 days, you can no longer contest, and on the 25th day you will receive a notice requesting payment along with a fine equal to the ticket. Act fast!
  • If you are dissatisfied with the Administrative Law Officer's decision, you can appeal before a judge under Administrative Review in the Circuit Court of Cook County located at the Daley Center, 50 W. Washington St., Room 602. This option must be exercised within 35 days of the Administrative Law Officer's decision.

What do I do?

How well you understand your case, and how much you prepare for it, can determine if your liable or not liable.

  1. Understand the defenses you can present. The following are, in simpler terms, instances in which you are not responsible for paying the ticket:
    1. You did not own the car when you received the ticket
    2. The vehicle was stolen, or license plates were stolen when you received the ticket
    3. There were no signs, or they weren’t clear enough, to stop you from parking*
    4. If there was a parking meter and it didn’t work
    5. The parking ticket incorrectly states a violation, or there is no evidence of it
    6. You were ticketed for something that wasn’t against the rules at the time you received the ticket
  2. Gather Evidence. Save any photos and videos you may have, have an accurate description of the scene, and save your 10 digit ticket number. The City of Chicago website allows you to see photos of your parking violation if you weren’t able to get any right away.
  3. You’ve submitted a request for a hearing and you’ve gathered your evidence. Now you have to present your case. The goal is to address the following:
    1. Start with a formal salutation to the judge, such as “Good morning, Your Honor.”
    2. Why are you there
    3. The circumstances in which you received the ticket (time, location, environment, etc.)
    4. What you were ticketed for
    5. Why you shouldn't be ticketed for that
      1. Address one of the allowable defenses presented above
      2. Present evidence

*In Ward 15, 3 of the top 5 violations are related to some sort of signage (Special Event, Improper Display, Parking Prohibited Anytime)

Example Appeal

Here is what you may do if you got a ticket for parking during street cleaning

Good Morning, Your Honor. My name is John Smith. I am here to contest ticket number 0000112233, which I received on December 3rd on Lincoln Ave. I found a spot on the street as the parking lot was full, and I made sure to follow the signs. I was ticketed for parking during “Street Cleaning”, but the spot I parked in had no legible signs . I shouldn’t be ticketed because according to Municipal Code, I should not get a ticket if a sign is not legible. In this picture here, you can see, Your Honor, that the one sign that was there was torn and the timings were not visible. I ask that my ticket be dismissed. Thank you, Your Honor.
